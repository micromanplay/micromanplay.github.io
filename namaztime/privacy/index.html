We practise data minimisation. The App does not collect a name, email, phone number, or any account identifier, because there is no account.

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not operate any analytics or tracking backend. However, because the free version shows Google ads, the AdMob SDK does collect and share an advertising identifier, IP address and limited device information with Google to serve those ads. We disclose this honestly here and in the Google Play Data Safety section, and this Policy and that form are kept consistent.

Because the App’s purpose reveals that you are a Muslim, we treat this as sensitive and minimise accordingly: ads are non-personalised (no behavioural/interest profiling), we share no in-app activity, and your calculation method/madhab are kept on-device only and never sent to Google.

If and when an ad-free option is offered, that version will not load the AdMob SDK and therefore will not collect or share the advertising identifier.

3. Permissions we request and why

  • Location (coarse/fine, one-shot, foreground only): to compute prayer times & Qibla. We do not request background location and the App does not track you in the background.
  • Exact alarms (USE_EXACT_ALARM / SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM): so the azan reminder fires at the exact prayer time — this is the App’s core function.
  • Notifications (POST_NOTIFICATIONS): to show the prayer reminder. Widget and lock-screen notification content is computed entirely on your device — nothing is sent anywhere. Defaults are generic (“Next prayer”) so your religious activity is not exposed to bystanders unless you opt in.
  • Foreground service (short): to briefly play the azan audio reliably.
  • Receive boot completed: to re-arm your prayer alarms after the phone restarts.

You can revoke any permission in Android Settings; some features (e.g. azan reminders) will stop working without the related permission.

4. Children (DPDP Act 2023 §9)

The App is a general-audience religious utility and is not directed at children, and we do not knowingly collect any personal data from children. Because we collect no age and no account, we cannot profile or behaviourally target anyone, including minors.

For advertising, the free version configures Google AdMob to treat traffic as under-the-age-of-consent / child-directed where applicable and to force non-personalised ads, and performs no behavioural tracking. We do not knowingly process a child’s personal data for tracking or targeted advertising.

5. Your rights (DPDP Act 2023)

As a Data Principal you may:

  • Access / know what data is used (this Policy describes all of it).
  • Correct your settings (editable in-app at any time).
  • Erase your data — because data lives on your device, you can erase it instantly via Android Settings → Apps → Namaz Time → Clear data or by uninstalling. Resetting your Advertising ID in Android Settings clears the ad identifier shared with Google.
  • Grieve — contact the Grievance Officer above; we respond within 30 days.
  • Withdraw consent — revoke location/notification permissions in Android Settings at any time, and stop the advertising-ID processing by tapping Android Settings → Privacy → Ads → Delete advertising ID (Android 12+). You can also re-open the ad-consent (UMP) options at any time in-app via More → “Ad privacy options”.

We do not transfer your personal data outside India through any service we operate. Google’s ad processing is governed by Google’s own policies and cross-border terms.

This Privacy Policy is governed by the laws of India and read together with our Terms of Use; the DPDP Act 2023 applies.

6. Security

The App stores your preferences and cached prayer times locally on your device using standard Android storage. We hold no central database of users, which materially reduces the risk of a data breach affecting you.

Personal-data-breach handling (DPDP Act 2023 §8(6)): in the unlikely event of a personal data breach affecting data we control, we will notify the Data Protection Board of India and each affected Data Principal in the form and manner prescribed under the Act, without undue delay. Because the App holds no central user database and prayer/location data lives only on your device, the realistic breach surface is limited to the advertising identifier processed by Google (governed by Google’s own breach obligations).
